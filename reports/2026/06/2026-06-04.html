Weight Compression New ANE-Optimized Quantization: Mixed-Precision INT4/INT8 per Layer

Apple's upcoming Core AI converter tools (expected at WWDC) reportedly fix the notorious fp16 overflow bugs that plagued Neural Engine deployments and introduce per-layer mixed-precision quantization. The practical pattern: attention layers stay at INT8 (preserving reasoning quality) while feed-forward layers compress to INT4 (saving 40% memory with minimal quality loss). For your existing MLX models, you can apply this today using mlx_lm.convert --mixed-precision flag — the converter analyzes each layer's sensitivity and assigns quantization levels automatically. Market Structure The $1.86B Liquidation Cascade — Why DeFi Needs Circuit Breakers

This week's $1.86B liquidation event — the largest single-day cascade in 15 months — exposes a structural weakness in crypto market microstructure: the absence of circuit breakers. Traditional exchanges halt trading when prices move too fast; crypto markets amplify moves via cascading liquidations that trigger more liquidations in a positive feedback loop. The emerging solution: progressive liquidation protocols that partially unwind positions over minutes rather than milliseconds, and "soft circuit breakers" that temporarily increase collateral requirements during volatility spikes rather than fully halting markets. Projects like Euler v2 and Morpho Blue are implementing these mechanisms. For DeFi developers: if you're building lending or perp protocols, progressive liquidation isn't optional — it's the difference between your protocol surviving a crash and creating systemic contagion.
