Emerging Context Engineering — The Discipline That Replaced "Prompt Engineering"

The hottest paradigm shift in AI development this week: Context Engineering is rapidly displacing "prompt engineering" as the core skill for building reliable AI systems. The distinction: prompt engineering focuses on crafting the perfect instruction text. Context engineering focuses on everything the model sees — system prompts, retrieved documents, tool definitions, conversation history, memory files, and environmental state. It's the engineering discipline of building dynamic systems that assemble the right context at the right time. For agent architects: your CLAUDE.md files, MCP tool schemas, memory configurations, and retrieval pipelines are all context engineering. DeFi Risk LRT Composability Risk — When Liquid Restaking Tokens Become Systemic Contagion Vectors

The rsETH/KelpDAO exploit crystallizes a structural risk the DeFi ecosystem has been ignoring: Liquid Restaking Tokens (LRTs) are composability time bombs. The pattern: LRTs like rsETH, ezETH, and pufETH are used as collateral across dozens of lending protocols simultaneously. A single oracle manipulation or depeg event cascades instantly across the entire DeFi stack because every protocol references the same underlying asset. Unlike traditional stETH (backed by established Lido infrastructure), newer LRTs have thinner liquidity, less battle-tested smart contracts, and complex redemption mechanics that break under stress. The architectural lesson for protocol developers: implement isolation modes for LRT collateral (separate risk pools), circuit breakers that pause lending when utilization spikes above 90%, and time-weighted oracles that resist flash manipulation.
