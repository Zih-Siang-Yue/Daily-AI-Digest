Regulation · Crypto The "Digital Asset Market Clarity Act" — 200+ Firms Push for Floor Vote

A bipartisan coalition of 200+ crypto firms — including Coinbase, Ripple, a16z Crypto, and Binance.US — sent a joint letter to Congressional leadership demanding an immediate floor vote on the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act (DAMCA). Bitcoin · Mining The Hashrate Bear Market — When Mining Economics Break Down

Today's 10.7% difficulty drop crystallizes a concept every Bitcoin-adjacent engineer should understand: the Hashrate Bear Market. Unlike price bear markets (driven by sentiment), hashrate bear markets are mechanistic — they occur when the spot price falls below the all-in production cost for a significant portion of the network. The current break-even for S19j Pro miners at US industrial power rates ($0.06/kWh) is approximately $58,000/BTC. With BTC at ~$62,400, margins are razor-thin. Historically, hashrate bear markets end in one of two ways: (1) price recovery above the miner cost curve (bullish), or (2) prolonged miner attrition until the network is dominated by the most efficient operators (bearish-then-bullish). The key metric to watch: the Hash Ribbon indicator — when the 30-day SMA of hashrate crosses back above the 60-day SMA after a capitulation event, it has been one of Bitcoin's most reliable buy signals. We're currently in the "capitulation" phase; the cross is the signal to watch.
