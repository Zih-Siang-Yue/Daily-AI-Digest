The Mining Death Spiral Risk

ThesisGame Theory

When 20% of miners operate at a loss and sell more BTC than they produce, a reflexive dynamic emerges: selling depresses price → more miners become unprofitable → more forced selling → further price decline. Historically this "death spiral" fear has never fully materialized because difficulty adjustments reduce competition for remaining miners. But the current cycle is different — miners carry AI-infrastructure debt from pivot investments that can't be serviced by mining alone. The question becomes: does the difficulty adjustment mechanism work fast enough to prevent a confidence crisis in network security?
