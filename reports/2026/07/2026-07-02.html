Neural Engine · Profiling ANE Utilization Monitoring: Know When Your Model Falls Off the Neural Engine

Most iOS developers ship ML models without knowing if they actually run on the Neural Engine or silently fall back to CPU. The diagnostic pattern: (1) In Xcode Instruments, add the "Core ML" instrument to your profiling session. (2) Run your inference code — the timeline shows per-layer compute unit assignment (ANE vs GPU vs CPU). (3) Any layer that falls to CPU appears as a red bar — common culprits: dynamic shapes, unsupported activations (GELU approximation variants), and non-power-of-2 tensor dimensions. (4) Fix strategy: reshape tensors to power-of-2 dims, replace unsupported ops with ANE-friendly equivalents (e.g., SiLU → sigmoid * x), and set MLComputeUnits.cpuAndNeuralEngine to prevent GPU fallback entirely.
