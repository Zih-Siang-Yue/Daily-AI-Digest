🔥 Hot Industry Concepts
Trending LLM-Agnostic Architecture: Decoupling AI Logic from Business Rules

A critical architectural pattern gaining momentum in July 2026: designing your AI integration layer to be completely model-agnostic. With OpenAI merging products, Anthropic extending limits, and models leapfrogging each other monthly, teams that hardcode a specific provider's API face constant migration pain. The pattern: (1) Define an AI Client protocol/interface that abstracts model-specific details — your business logic calls aiClient.complete(prompt:) without knowing if it's hitting Claude, GPT, or Gemini underneath. (2) Configuration-driven model routing: a JSON/YAML config maps task types to models: {"code_review": "claude-sonnet-5", "autocomplete": "gpt-5.6-luna", "translation": "glm-5.2"}. Swap providers without code changes. (3) Response normalization: each provider returns slightly different formats — your adapter layer normalizes to a single internal format before it reaches business logic. (4) Fallback chains: if your primary model is down or rate-limited, automatically route to the secondary. For iOS developers: build your on-device AI features behind a ModelProvider protocol today.
